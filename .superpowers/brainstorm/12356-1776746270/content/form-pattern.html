How should "Run Analysis" feel to use?

The form is where the farmer actually works. Three patterns, same inputs — very different cognitive load.

PROMPT
Kimberly Farm, sandy loam alfalfa, heat coming tomorrow — how much should I irrigate?
AI inferred 9 fields · edit ▸
soil: sandy loam ✓ crop: alfalfa ✓ temp 97°F ⚡

A — Prompt-first (AI does the work)

Big textarea is the hero. Farmer types a sentence; AI extracts soil, crop, weather, timing as chips below. Click a chip to correct. Form fields collapse by default — only appear if you open "details".

Best for: daily use once trust is built. Risk: feels magical but can feel opaque if extraction is wrong.

B — Guided Wizard

Four short steps: Field → Conditions → Constraints → Review. Progress bar shows where you are. Only 2–4 fields visible at a time. Never scroll past what you need right now.

Best for: first-time users, infrequent use. Risk: power users find step-clicking tedious.

C — Compact Stacked Cards

Three themed sections (Field / Conditions / Irrigation) as slim cards. Each shows a summary line when collapsed; expands inline when you tap. Everything on one screen, no hidden state.

Best for: experts who tweak one thing. Risk: still feels form-y, just prettier.

A is the most "frontier AI" — the form melts away. B is the most farmer-friendly for first contact. C is the safest middle.
